"Is Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) A Clustering Algorithm?"; by Giri Rabindranath (https://girirabi.medium.com); Nov 2, 2023;

'Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA)' is a form of unsupervised learning, and it is used for discovering hidden groupings within data.

Groupings are topics which fit natural language processing and text analysis use cases.

LDA ascribes a distribution of topics over a data set vs a single topic.

Clustering algorithms use semantic distance measures to assign a similarity between data within a cluster.

LDA ascribes a frequency count of the data grams (aka words) in the documents to determine similarity within a topic.
