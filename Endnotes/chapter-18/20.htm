"Elsevier Semantic Information Engine (ELSSIE)"; 2018  Patent EFS ID: 27936000; Application Number: 62440447 ; January 1, 2016;

In this pending US patent, semantic modeling is operationalized in an engine for Elsevier's rollout.

The embodiments provide a best practice for the fields of: scientific research publication, human learning psychology, computational linguistics, (semantic) knowledge engineering, computer science, (algorithmic) graph theory and the deep domain fields of: chemistry, biology, and pharmaceutical research.

The goal of the work was to flesh out a system for initially processing Life Science publications into an organized semantic information processing system to enable researchers to mine for information and gain new insights in a formalized framework. The unification of the deep semantic knowledge across various life science research fields would make it possible for a machine to understand a domain of expertise and to relate it to various other domains while providing a unified view of the cognitive structure of that information. The researcher problem is:

 

"There is too much information for any one individual to process in a life-time much less within the scope of a given research effort" - Source: Richard Schiller, ELSSIE's abstract, 2016

 

As described in detail below, embodiments provide:

1.      structured and unstructured subject matter expert produced publications ingested as facts, organized semantically via advanced unstructured / structure data processing;

2.      next generation semantic knowledge engineering with advanced machine learning; and

3.      information search, mining, quantitative analytics: are three core subsystems that the systems leverage in its delivery of this vision.
