"A Technical Analysis of Confidential Computing"; by the Confidential Computing Consortium; November 2022;

In this whitepaper you are provided an overview of how Confidential Computing addresses this problem below, along with use cases and motivation:

1.      Data exists in three states: in data-in-transit, data-at-rest, and in data-in-use (protecting sensitive data in all of its states is more critical);

2.      Cryptography is deployed to provide data confidentiality and integrity (tampering . . . protecting data in transit and at rest are common . . .  protecting data in use is NOT);

3.      Hardware based confidential computing in Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs) protects sensitive data / code against threats execution that were previously difficult, if not impossible, to protect against (confidential computing protects data against adversarial owners); and

4.      With increased protection for data-in-use, new use cases are possible (i.e., multi-party computations in financial services, regulated industries, or machine learning at the edge where the data being operated on needs protecting from the processing environment owner itself).
