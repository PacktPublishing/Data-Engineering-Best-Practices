"Layered Security: Think Castle Walls and a Moat Full of Alligators (Castle Defense)"; 2024;

"Smart castle builders in the medieval ages knew the value of layered security and created multiple hurdles to challenge attackers. Deep moats, often rumored to be filled with alligators, prevented invaders from tunneling under the castle. Towering double walls withstood the hurl of battering rams and catapults. High walk walls were used to sling arrows and other direct attack methods to prevent breaches into the castles core." - Source E.O. Johnson; 2023

As with a castle, layered security makes it possible for you to protect sensitive core data with protective surrounding walls. Layered security may look like:

1.      A perpetrator impersonating a CEO by issuing an email with questions asking employees to click on a malicious link or attachment.

2.      In the first layer of defense an anti-spam solution detects the email and preventing its ever appearing in your email inbox.

3.      A second layer is activated if your anti-spam software doesn't catch the malicious activity and your local workstations virus protection software quarantines the malicious software the email tried to activate.

4.    Then a third layer of defense should protect against activity like a Zero-Day  21 attack, but there is no known remedy for this yet.  The malicious actor has now wreaked havoc out your enterprise data; this activity results in a Zero-Day Exploit, or Zero-Day Attack.

Only an Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) / Intrusion Prevention System (IPS) from companies like Juniper can resolve the active exploit attack by heuristic analysis of the vector.
