StranglerFigApplication, by Martin Fowler, June 29, 2004;

refer to: https://www.martinfowler.com/bliki/StranglerApplication.html

In Australia there are huge strangler fig trees.

Seeding begins in upper branches and gradually work down until rooted in soil.

Over time they grow into odd shapes while strangling and killing the host tree.

Fowler advocates for gradually creating a new system around the edges of the old, letting the new system grow slowly over several years until the old system is strangled, just like the strangler fig.
