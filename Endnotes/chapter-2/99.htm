Oracle Data Marketplace; 2024; was a third-party data marketplace and standard for open, transparent audience data trading. It shut down in September 2024.

The "Oracle Data Marketplace" was an ecosystem built using quality data, flexible and fair pricing which scaled. It had comprehensive access to data to target audiences in the purchase funnel. And it provided >30,000 data attributes for branding and direct marketing use cases; and is used by >300 million users. The marketplace ran on the "Data Activation System" for data management and analytics. 80% of the top 20 ad networks, portals, trading desks, and creative optimizers leveraged the platform to run ad campaigns.

The issue was that it was subject to at least two class action lawsuits alleging that Oracle violated user privacy. The claims identified that Oracle maintained "hidden dossiers" on ~5 billion entities, and that Oracle collected and sold this personal, behavioral, and historical data to others. This is a violation of the Federal Electronic Communications Privacy Act, and the California Invasion of Privacy Act among other laws. The allegation of surveillance on the part of the Oracle product which tracked and collected users data in real-time without consent using cookies, tracking pixels, and other methods was a gross violation of the law. The data collected included personal information, behavioral data, income, political views, and hobbies.

Notwithstanding these collection methods being a cause for product shutdown, if the legal and consent issues were handled better and aligned to correct security privacy, distribution rights and ethical conduct the product idea would be and should be sound for reactivation elsewhere but under the correct controls.
