Heuristic Algorithms for Real-World Applications: Success Stories and Future Directions; by Dr. Vivek Pandey (https://www.linkedin.com/in/dr-vivek-pandey) ; June 13, 2023; as described by Vivek, heuristic algorithms use approximations to find optimal solutions to complex problems. The approach works in phases:

1.      problem formulation;

2.      solution representation;

3.      heuristic selection;

4.      search and optimization; and

5.      solution evaluation and refinement.

Heuristic data preprocessing is described for the following fields: agriculture, e-commerce, education, energy, energy management, finance, gaming, healthcare, logistics, manufacturing, medical diagnostics, and transportation. An example for healthcare follows:

"Data collection and pre-processing: Once the problem has been defined, the next step is to collect and pre-process data. This may involve gathering medical images, patient records, and other relevant data from various sources. The data may also need to be cleaned, standardized, and normalized to ensure consistency and accuracy. "
