ML Model Drift is the reduction of a ML models quality over time as a result of natural changes in the data set. The changes are evidence as the context the data was supplied in changes along with the class relationships. Two types exist:

1.      Concept Drift means that the properties of the data class are changing. The tuned algorithm that ML modeled features fails to meet its quality threshold. This is seen in email spam filters that need to evolve quickly to address the malicious innovative capability of the email producer.

2.      Data Drift occurs when the underlying statistical distribution of the features necessary for the ML model erodes over time. Calendaring impacts often lead to this data drift where during some timeframes expected feature values are just missing.
