"What is TRiSM?"; by Technopedia; 2024;

TRiSM stands for trust, risk, and security management.

Popular TRiSM frameworks include:

1.      Gartner Trust, Risk and Security Management (TRiSM) framework is an approach to manage: trust, risk, and security in AI systems. Guidelines and best practices are provided to ensure: ethical, fair, reliable, and secure. See "Tackling Trust, Risk and Security in AI Models" (https://www.gartner.com/en/articles/what-it-takes-to-make-ai-safe-and-effective).

2.      National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) has an AI risk management framework strategy to identify, assess, and mitigate AI system risks covering: data privacy, security, and bias. See "NIST: AI Risk Management Framework" (https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework).

3.      Microsoft's Responsible AI Framework provides guidance in regard to how to develop and deploy accountable ethical AI systems addressing: bias, fairness, transparency, and privacy. See: "Microsofts Responsible AI Standard, V2 General Requirements; June 2022" (https://blogs.microsoft.com/wp-content/uploads/prod/sites/5/2022/06/Microsoft-Responsible-AI-Standard-v2-General-Requirements-3.pdf).

4.      Google AI Principles drive guidelines for AI development and deployment. They emphasize: fairness, privacy, and security in AI systems. See "Googles AI Responsibility Principles" (https://ai.google/responsibility/principles).

5.      World Economic Forum (WEF) Principles of Responsible AI is a framework with recommendations for how to acquire AI systems that are: ethical, fair, and accountable. They were developed by international experts, covering topics like: bias, fairness, transparency, privacy, and security. See "World Economic Forum: Adopting AI Responsibly: Guidelines for Procurement of AI Solutions by the Private Sector; Insight Report;June 2023" (https://www3.weforum.org/docs/WEF_Adopting_AI_Responsibly_Guidelines_for_Procurement_of_AI_Solutions_by_the_Private_Sector_2023.pdf).
