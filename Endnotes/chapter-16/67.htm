DSPy; 2024;

DSPy is a framework for algorithmically optimizing language model (LM) prompts and weights.

It is used when LMs are called one or more times within a MLOps pipeline.

The process to build a complex system without DSPy, involves:

1.      Breaking the problem down into steps;

2.      Creating prompts for your LM until each step works well in isolation;

3.      Tweaking each step to work well as a unified solution;

4.      Generating synthetic examples to tune each of the steps; and

5.      Using these examples to break down the grand LM into finer tuned smaller LMs in order to cut costs.

Tunning and LM is hard, time consuming and subject to change as pipeline, your LM, or your data change since prompts (or finetuning) is always in flux.

DSPy does two things:

1.      It separates program flow from the parameters (aka LM prompts and LM weights) for each step;  and

2.      Adds new optimizers, which are themselves LM driven algorithms to tune the LM prompts and/or the LM Weights made with your LM calls for the target metric that you want to maximize.

Refer to https://github.com/stanfordnlp/dspy.
