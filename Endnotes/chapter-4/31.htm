SHACL vs ShEx Comparison; 2023; ShEx means 'Shape Expressions Language 2.1' and is not a W3C track. ShEx is a community group effort.

It describes RDF nodes and graph structures. A node constraint describes the RDF node (IRI, blank node or literal).

A shape describes triples involving nodes of an RDF graph. Predicates, associated cardinalities, and datatypes are described.

ShEx shapes may be used to inform one about data structures associated with processes or interfaces, generate, or validate data, or drive user interfaces.

Refer to: https://www.w3.org/2014/data-shapes/wiki/SHACL-ShEx-Comparison.
