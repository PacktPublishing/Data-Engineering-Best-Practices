In 1936, the Michelin Guides three star award system was published.

The criteria of the rating system are awarded based on five different criteria:

1.      Quality of ingredients;

2.      Mastery of flavor / cooking technique;

3.      Personality of the cuisine's chef;

4.      The value for money; and

5.      Consistency of food;

The number of stars indicate:

1.      1-Star: quality cooking / worth a stop;

2.      2-Stars: exceptional cooking / worth a detour; and

3.      3-Stars: unique cuisine / worth a special trip.
