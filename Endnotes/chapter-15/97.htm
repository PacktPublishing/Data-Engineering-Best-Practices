"Zeppelin v.s. Jupyter - a Comparison from a Different Perspective"; by Liangjun Jiang (https://liangjunjiang.medium.com); May 29, 2020;

In this article the author askes a few questions related to these two notebook types:

1.       Who are you? Are you a Data Scientist with first-hand tool experience, or an engineer deploying tools for the Data Scientist, or a software engineer?

2.       What do you use this tool for, as a data exploratory tool to interact with data, or to develop data applications?

3.       How trained are you with ither tool, are you good enough to give expert- or at least some degree of professional suggestion?

The project mission statements for these notebooks are identified as follows and it is pointed out that the mission statements don't lead you to a clear understanding of the capability overlaps:

1.       Project Jupyter exists to develop open-source software, open-standards, and services for interactive computing across dozens of programming languages. Source: https://jupyter.org.

2.       Apache Zeppelin web-based notebook that enables data-driven, interactive data analytics and collaborative documents with SQL, Scala and more. Source: https://zeppelin.apache.org.
